# Foraging Ethics

*The community agreement every contributor and user operates under.*

Foraging is older than property lines. But today it happens inside them — on public sidewalks, in city parks, along bike paths, at the edges of someone's yard. Getting this right matters. These are the rules the Forage for All community commits to, in code, copy, and moderation.

---

## The core rule

> **Take a third. Leave a third for the birds. Leave a third for the earth.**

If a tree has 30 plums, take 10 at most. Birds and insects need their share. Seeds need to fall for next year. Other foragers are coming after you.

---

## What you can pin

✅ **Pin freely:**
- Street trees on public sidewalks
- Parks with no harvesting ban posted
- Trails and public right-of-ways
- Abandoned lots (with care — check ownership)
- Your own property, if you want to share

❌ **Never pin:**
- Private property without explicit owner permission
- Community gardens without the group's consent
- Protected or restored habitat
- Agricultural fields (that's theft, not foraging)
- Parks with posted no-harvest rules

⚠️ **Flag with warnings:**
- Roadside finds (within ~30ft of traffic — lead, exhaust)
- Industrial-adjacent (former gas stations, rail lines, factories)
- Sprayed areas (parks using herbicides, golf-course edges)
- Dog-walk zones (urine contamination on low fruit/greens)

---

## Species we won't map

Some species are too sensitive for a public map. Moderators blocklist:

- **Rare or threatened plants** (ramps in overpicked regions, ginseng, etc.)
- **Mushrooms in critical-habitat zones** — location-sharing can destroy populations
- **Species under local legal protection** — varies by region, moderator discretion

If you're unsure, pin it **private** (visible only to you) and ask a moderator.

---

## Identification

**Never pin a species you aren't certain about.** A wrong ID on a public map can poison someone.

- Cross-check with iNaturalist, GBIF, or a regional field guide before confirming.
- If in doubt, pin it as "unknown edible" with a photo and ask the community.
- Toxic look-alikes are flagged in the catalog — read the warning before you harvest.
- We do not ship AI plant-ID. Humans pick species from the curated list.

---

## Harvesting

- **Tools over hands** — use scissors or pruners to avoid damaging branches
- **Leave no trace** — pack out trimmings, close gates, don't trample
- **Don't block** — sidewalks, driveways, trails while you pick
- **Greet neighbors** — if you're picking at someone's fenceline, knock first
- **No ladders** in public parks unless you have permission

---

## Community conduct

- Confirm pins honestly. Don't say "ripe" when it isn't.
- Don't mark pins "gone" to keep them for yourself.
- Don't flag pins out of spite or territoriality.
- Moderation is done by volunteers. Be kind when appealing decisions.

---

## Reporting

See a pin that breaks these rules? Tap **⋯ → Flag** in the app, or open an issue tagged [`ethics`](https://github.com/forage-for-all/app/labels/ethics) on GitHub.

Repeat offenders lose posting privileges.
