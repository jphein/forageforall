# Press Kit

Everything journalists, bloggers, and partners need to cover Forage for All.

## Boilerplate

**One-liner:**
> A free, open-source map of fruit trees and edible plants growing on public land.

**Short (50 words):**
> Forage for All is a free, volunteer-run mobile app that maps fruit trees, berries, and edible plants on public land. Users drop pins, share ripeness updates, and discover what's growing in their own neighborhood. Licensed AGPLv3, with no ads, no trackers, and no selling of location data.

**Long (~100 words):**
> Forage for All is an open-source iOS and Android app that turns public edible plants — street fruit, park berries, trailside nuts — into a living community map. Users drop pins, upload photos, and confirm when fruit is ripe, producing a time-aware atlas of local food that would otherwise go to waste. Built by volunteers on Expo, InstantDB, and Google Maps, the project is licensed under AGPLv3 with privacy as a founding principle: coordinates are fuzzed by default, there are no ads or analytics, and user data is never sold. It launched in 2026 as a non-commercial public good.

## Facts

- **Founded:** 2026
- **License:** AGPL-3.0-or-later
- **Stack:** Expo, React Native, TypeScript, InstantDB, Google Maps
- **Platforms:** iOS 15+, Android 10+
- **Pricing:** Free, forever
- **Funding:** Volunteer time; donations accepted (public ledger)

## Key differentiators

- **Open source.** Source is auditable and forkable. AGPLv3 prevents closed commercial resale of community data.
- **Privacy-first.** Fuzzy locations by default. No analytics SDKs. No trackers.
- **Ethics-led.** Community foraging ethics built into the product (take-a-third rule, sensitive-species blocklists).
- **Community-owned.** No VC, no exit strategy, no growth obligations.

- Imply we take VC funding
